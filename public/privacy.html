Privacy Policy

How QRMeet handles your data.

What is stored on your device — A pseudonymous game identifier, an authentication token, your chosen display name, emoji, and current room are saved in your browser's local storage (not cookies). If you organise rooms, their IDs and admin tokens are kept separately in an admin keychain. Resetting the game from the About screen erases your player session; the admin keychain is preserved so you stay signed in to the rooms you manage.

Nothing is stored until you join — Opening a QRMeet link (a scan, a treasure, or a room link) no longer registers you automatically. No profile is created — on your device or on our servers — until you explicitly confirm that you want to enter the game.

Why we keep a unique identifier — This pseudonymous identifier is what lets the app recognise you throughout an event — remembering the people you meet and keeping your score. It is not linked to your real-world identity. Storing it is strictly necessary to deliver the core functionality you have requested: without it the service simply cannot work, and it is processed on that legal basis (performance of the service). It is never used for advertising, profiling, or tracking you across other sites.

What is stored on our servers — Your pseudonymous identifier, chosen display name, emoji, room membership, encounter history (who met whom and when), a rotating single-use QR token, and a salted hash of your IP address are stored in a Cloudflare database for the lifetime of the room — by default one week from its creation — after which they are automatically and permanently deleted. The exact deletion time is shown as a countdown on the room's board and admin pages.

Passkeys (optional) — On devices that support it, QRMeet offers to protect your profile with a passkey (Face ID / Touch ID), so you can recover it from another browser on the same device. You can decline the system prompt and play normally. What our servers store is only the passkey's public key and random identifiers — never your biometric data, which stays on your device and is never transmitted. The link between a passkey and a room's profile is deleted with the room; the public key itself is kept so the same passkey keeps working at future events, and is automatically deleted after about a year without use.

IP address — Your IP address is never stored in clear text. A salted, one-way hash of it is kept on your account for the lifetime of the room, so that the room's organiser can see a short fragment of that hash to spot duplicate or automated accounts. The raw address is used only in-memory to serve your requests and is never stored or shown in readable form.

Cloudflare — This app runs on Cloudflare Workers and its CDN. Cloudflare automatically injects its own Web Analytics, which is cookieless: it gathers aggregated, anonymous traffic statistics without setting any tracking cookie or identifying you. Cloudflare processes request metadata (IP, user-agent, country) as a data processor and may set one or more strictly-necessary functional cookies (e.g. for bot protection or security challenges); these are required for the service to operate and are not used for tracking. See Cloudflare's Privacy Policy.

No third-party trackers. No advertising network or social-media pixel is embedded, and — apart from Cloudflare's own cookieless analytics described above — no third-party analytics SDK is used in this app.
